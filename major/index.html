Honorable Mentions

I would like to dedicate this section to a few astronomers that were originally going to be on this website but did not receive their own section in the end:

  1. Henrietta Swan Leavitt for her discovery of Cepheid variable stars
  2. Williamina Paton Stevens Fleming for her discovery of RR Lyrae variable stars (she actually made it in the banner)
  3. Annie Jump Cannon for her classification of stars still used today

The people in the banner, listed from left to right, are: Edwin Hubble, Max Wolf, Williamina Paton Stevens Fleming, and Johann Franz Encke.

Get Involved

There are many ways to learn more about space. One way is to visit space websites, such as NASA, space.com.

Another way to get involved is to join a local group dedicated to space. Are you a student at Western University? If so, join the Space Society of London! Their page is here.

Do you have an interest in comets and asteroids? Find out more about Professor Peter Brown, who is researching these topics, here. You can also contact him through his email here.

Recommendation

If you like watching animated shows or reading comic books (especially Japanese ones), there is one called "Asteroid in Love" that teaches viewers all about astronomy and earth sciences. My love for many scientific areas started from watching it.

The story is about the journey of the main characters, Mira and Ao, as they try to fulfill their dream of finding and naming an asteroid, as well as their activities in their earth science club.

Many of the images on this website come from it (even the font was chosen due to its similarity with the one used in the show). If this interests you at all, please read or watch it.
