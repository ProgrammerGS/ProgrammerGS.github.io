Art Jam Animation

A Short Blender Animation

Overview

I created this animation for the GDS Art Jam 2025, where it won first place. The scene depicts a girl dressed for Halloween, gazing sadly out her window at the rain, unable to go trick‑or‑treating. All assets were produced during the jam, and I completed the project solo — from concept and scene planning to modeling, animation, lighting, and final rendering. I chose to submit a full animation rather than a still image, which set my work apart. The project strengthened my skills in Blender and gave me confidence in my abilities, marking my first event win.

  • Blender — modeling, animation, lighting, and rendering
  • Scene Planning — designed room layout, props, and atmosphere
  • Animation — blinking character, rain effects, and lightning/thunder
  • Solo Project Execution — handled all stages from concept to final submission
  • Event Achievement — won first place at GDS Art Jam 2025 Announcement shared in the club’s Discord community

Creative Highlights

  • Hidden storytelling details — subtle objects in the scene carry meaning:
    • The circled calendar date emphasizes anticipation for Halloween.
    • The empty trick‑or‑treat bag shows her excitement and disappointment.
    • The wand placed aside reflects her loss of mood to play.
    • The jack‑o’‑lantern reinforces the Halloween theme and atmosphere.
  • Use of lighting — three distinct sources create mood:
    • Jack‑o’‑lantern glow behind the girl.
    • Offscreen streetlight through the window.
    • Lightning flashes that transform the scene dynamically.
  • Atmospheric rendering — depth of field and volumetric fog enhance realism and mood, guiding focus to the character while softening the environment.
  • Balance of negative space — deliberate emptiness (bare shelf, character’s stillness) enhances the somber tone without clutter.
  • Simplistic animation & camera — static camera, minimal character movement, and restrained effects keep focus on atmosphere rather than spectacle.
  • Efficient scene planning — only modeled what was visible (upper body, room objects, offscreen light source), saving time while maintaining impact.
