The Girl Under the Tree

A 3D Anomaly Puzzle Game

Overview

This game was developed for GDSxLoJam 2026 as a 3D puzzle experience where players search for anomalies to escape a forest. Since the jam, I have continued polishing the game to its current state, which now includes multilingual support. The current version features 21 anomalies and two distinct endings, each accompanied by animated cutscenes and ending screens rendered in Blender. Beyond anomalies, the main NPC responds dynamically to the player — smiling when looked at, waving from a distance, and even playing with an apple during a related anomaly — adding subtle interactive depth. All 3D assets were created in Blender, and the entire codebase was implemented in Godot.

  • Godot Engine — implemented the entire game codebase
  • Blender — created all 3D assets and rendered cutscenes
  • 3D Modeling & Animation — designed characters, environments, and animated sequences
  • Procedural Animation — applied advanced animation techniques for dynamic effects
  • Multilingual Support — integrated English, Japanese, and Chinese translations
  • Game Design — developed puzzle mechanics and anomaly system
  • Cutscene Production — created animated endings with Blender rendering

Challenges & Learnings

During development, one of the biggest challenges was creating cutscenes that felt visually compelling. In the original game jam build, I produced the ending cutscene by posing characters directly in Godot. After reflecting on the result, I decided to re‑animate the sequence in Blender, where I had stronger control over lighting and scene composition. This shift wasn’t easy — I encountered multiple rendering issues and went through extensive trial‑and‑error before achieving a look I was satisfied with. Ultimately, the process gave me valuable experience in cinematic scene creation with Blender and reinforced the importance of persistence: experimenting across tools, troubleshooting problems, and iterating until the final cutscene matched the quality I envisioned.

  • Explored multiple tools — experimented with both Godot and Blender for cutscene creation
  • Improved lighting & composition — leveraged Blender’s strengths to achieve more cinematic visuals
  • Overcame rendering issues — solved technical challenges through trial‑and‑error
  • Learned persistence — reinforced the value of not giving up when facing setbacks
